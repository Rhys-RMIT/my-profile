The Smart Garden


Since I like nature and wellbeing, I have been interested in creating a smart garden. This smart garden should be able to be controlled by an app through a mobile device. The smart garden would be about twice the size of a refrigerator and could potentially be the next generation whitegoods product for millions of homes around the world and help to reduce hunger and starvation.

To introduce this as a standard in our homes we would need to work with architects to develop spaces within common home plans so that these can be purchased and placed in a relevant area of the home.

The Smart Garden can also be placed indoors or outdoors with two different models.

The indoor model would be much like a refrigerator and include transparent glass windows so that the owner can easily see inside the smart garden. It would be aesthetically designed and pleasing to customers. The indoor model would also include internal blinds to reduce light from the hydroponic lamp system if the lights were on at night or the user does not want to see the plants.

The outdoor model would be more have an outdoor furniture design yet be durable enough to handle all weather conditions. Windows would automatically open when needed for fresh air in the summer and closed to create a greenhouse during the winter. The outdoor option also helps against rodents and other animals eating the plants.

Owners may wish to buy several of the indoor and outdoor smart gardens, and they can be linked together as desired.

How the Smart Garden works, would be connected to the home water and electrical system. A program would be developed to check the plants, fruit and vegetables being grown. Regulate temperatures, control water flow and lighting for optimal growth. We would develop an online website and an app that could control the smart garden remotely through Arduino hardware and sensors, actuators, motors, and cameras for optimal plant growth.

There are some of these already existing in the market today, however the problem I find with these others are they are not sustainable. For example, the smart garden should be able to grow and produce enough green food for a small family in an average family home space.

Flight Navigation System (FNS)


The Flight Navigation System (FNS) is a system designed for the future of traffic. In the not-too-distant future, the world will need a way to reduce traffic and that will occur through flight. The average consumer will be able to fly from his or her home to their chosen destination. The question is how will this be regulated and how will we develop the technology to enhance this experience?

The flight navigation system is an operating system that is connected to the vehicles computer and will control the navigation to a destination. The driver or pilot will control the vehicle using the control column or better known by gamers as a joystick to control the direction of the vehicle. The vehicle will not fly unless the pilot is also wearing a connected helmet. And the helmet will act as an augmented reality device where the pilot can visually see the road in front of the vehicle with augmented overlays producing flight paths and rules like road rules.

For example, some of the flight rules would need to be developed to create this technology and how it would be used effectively. A rule such as: How high above ground traffic should a flying vehicle be allowed to fly? Would be programmed into the FNS so that a pilot cannot fly too high and enter airplane airspace or too low where the vehicle may collide with a telegraph pole, trees, or building.

Another questionable rule would be: Should the flying vehicle be able to fly over existing road infrastructure? Since the roads are already mapped, they could keep the aircraft traffic in order effectively creating a second layer of traffic above ground traffic.

Flying Cars and Motorcycles


If we were to create new ways to transport humans, then we are going to need to look to the sky. The land is already congested around cities and towns. And that is why electric flying cars and motorcycles are going to be the next important thing for transportation. Especially when humans start to settle on Mars.

Developing a flying car or motorcycle would be an exciting project. A lot of the hardware such as flight controllers, electric motors and computer hardware technology have already been developed and can be implemented with good software development and design.

The flying car and motorcycle concept would need many sensors added to the vehicle. For the operating system to compute. Some of the sensors would need to be an ultrasonic or an infrared sensor to measure distance between objects and used to protect pilots against collisions. Another sensor would be a barometric pressure sensor to detect the altitude of a vehicle so that when the sensor detects that a pilot is flying too low or too high the vehicle safety system can automatically correct the error. Furthermore, a lidar system uses lasers to measure distances and are used in scanning the surrounding space to create a three-dimensional view for the computer to calculate difficult to see obstacles such as wires or birds.

The sensors would be controlled by good software programming such as the development of the Flight Navigation System (FNS) mentioned above for a mixture of automated tasks and pilot interaction. Human beings would need to have some form of control of the vehicle during takeoff and landing until a fully automated system can be trusted by the population.

This type of project would be most useful for when humans begin to live on mars. Mars is a smaller planet (slightly more than half the size of the earth) The surface gravity on Mars is less than the surface gravity on Earth. The surface gravity on Mars is only about 38% of the surface gravity on Earth, so if a vehicle weighed 1 ton on Earth, the vehicle would weigh about 380 kilograms on Mars. And this is why flying vehicles would be ideal on Mars.
